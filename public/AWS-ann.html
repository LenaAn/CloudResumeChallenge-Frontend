I am now an AWS Certified Cloud Practitioner!

For this you get the following link and now everyone can make sure that you have shown cloud fluency and foundational AWS knowledge. In addition, you can attach a badge to your LinkedIn page.

How did I prepare?

- Free courses on Aws SkillBuilder: -- Exam Prep: AWS Certified Cloud Practitioner (CLF-C01) — AWS Technical Essentials - Practice tests on udemy https://www.udemy.com/course/aws-certified-cloud-practitioner-practice-exams-c/ (Questions are slightly more difficult than in the real exam)

What does the exam check?

AWS Cloud Practitioner foundational exam. Whether you're a devOps developer or a sales person, this is a good start for everyone. Therefore, the content there is a bit general and there is something that needs to be taught specifically for the exam. Even if you have a lot of experience in projects on AWS, you hardly remember that the designated Technical Account manager is available in Enterprise support (which costs $15k per month). But basically, the test can be passed on common sense and experience - questions like what is agility, what determines the cost of EC2, what file system can be mounted simultaneously on several EC2 machines?

How is the exam going?

There are 65 questions in the exam, 50 of them count, 15 do not. These are new questions they are testing. What questions count, you don't know. The exam is given 90 minutes. For the exam, you can get from 100 to 1000 points, the passing score is 700. I passed 827. The exam costs $100. In some cities there is a testing center where you can come and pass. Otherwise, you can take the exam at home through Pearson Vue. You need a room where no one is distracting, before the start of the exam you need to clean the room, remove everything unnecessary from the table, remove additional monitors, notebooks, and in general everything suspicious. During the exam, the proctor looks at you through the camera and makes sure you don't cheat. You can’t leave the view of the camera, you can’t read questions aloud, and it’s better not to look anywhere except for the monitor (the only one!) If the risks are that the Internet will fall off in the process or the neighbors will be distracting, or the phone will ring, then the exam may not be counted.
